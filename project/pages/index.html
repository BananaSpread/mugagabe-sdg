The "Mugagabe SDGs" project aims to bring the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) directly into the classroom by leveraging NASA's vast resources and cutting-edge technologies. Through a dynamic e-learning platform, students aged 10-17 will have the opportunity to explore interactive projects that focus on key global challenges, such as climate action, clean energy, and sustainable cities. By linking SDG concepts to real-world NASA missions, satellite data, and scientific research, this project offers an engaging and hands-on approach to learning, empowering students to become active participants in building a more sustainable future.

At the heart of this initiative is a commitment to fostering collaboration within the school community and beyond. Through team-based activities, students, teachers, and families will work together on projects designed to tackle local challenges while connecting to the global SDG framework. By blending space exploration with sustainability education, this platform aims to inspire the next generation of changemakers who can think critically, act locally, and innovate globally in addressing our planet's most pressing environmental and social issues.
